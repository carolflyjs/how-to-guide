Welcome to the API How-to Guide

In this guide, you will learn the basics APIs and how to use APIs to get data that can enriches your website content. Note that the guide is designed for those who just started front-end web development with working knowledge about HTML. If you are an advanced web developer, this guide is not for you.

What is API ?

A.K.A Application Programming Interface

You are creating your own website about Chinese action movie stars like Bruce Lee or Jackie Chan. You decided to provide your visitors with information of their movies. While you do not want to send them away to other sites like IMDB, you are still hoping to take advantage of their data.

If this story sounds somewhat familiar, you are looking for the solution of "API." But firstly, what is API?

Like many others, your search landed you onto Wikipedia where API is defined as "a set of routines, protocols, and tools for building software and applications." It is a rather technical, boring and somewhat scary definition. But relax, API is fun and not difficult at all. Let's start with the concept of interface.

You came across a nice girl through an instant dating website on a Saturday afternoon. After messaging each other for a while, you decide to meet up right away! Now you just need to agree on where and how to recognize each other. So you decided to meet at Four Barrel Coffee, with you carrying a red book. On the date, you asked her questions about her job and family and made a hit! A successful first date!

Believe it or not, making API connections is just like a first date. Only that instead of two human beings, it involves two applications. They meet at a URI address (the Four Barrel Coffee), with identity information (the red book) and some queries of interest (job and family questions).

Doens't sound that difficult after all, does it? So now click the arrow below to move on!
