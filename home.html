Welcome to the How-to Guide

In this guide, you will learn how to use Wikipedia APIs to exchange data through your own website. Before starting, note that it is an entry-level API guide. However, it does assume that you have working knowledge about HTML, CSS, JavaScript and DOM. If you are an advanced web developer, this guide may not suit your needs.

What is Web API?

a.k.a. Application Programming Interface

You are creating your own website about Chinese action movie stars like Bruce Lee or Jackie Chan. You decided to provide your visitors with information of their movies. While you do not want to send them away to other sites like IMDB, you are still hoping to take advantage of their data.

If this story sounds somewhat familiar, you are looking for the solution of "Web API." But firstly, what is Web API?

Like many others, your search landed you onto Wikipedia where API is defined as "a set of routines, protocols, and tools for building software and applications." It is a rather technical, boring and somewhat scary definition. In fact, API is fun and not difficult at all. Let's start with a daily scenario that largely resembles API.

On a Saturday afternoon, you, a San Francisco-based 30-yeare-old man, meet a girl from Oakland on an instant dating website. After messaging each other for a while, you decide to meet up in the city! So you two agree on where to meet and how to recognize each other. The decision is to meet at Four Barrel Coffee, with you carrying a red book. On the date, you have a great chat. You ask each other about lives and she seemed to be the kind of girl you want to date! A successful first date!

Believe it or not, making web API connections is just like a first date. Only that instead of two human beings, it involves two applications. They meet at a URI address (the Four Barrel Coffee), with identity information (the red book) and bring each other queries of interest (questions about life).

Doesn't sound that difficult after all, does it? Now you know what web API is, so click the arrow below to move on!
